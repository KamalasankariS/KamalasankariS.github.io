YOLO: ROOF MATERIAL DETECTION USING AERIAL IMAGERY

IEEE ICCDS · 2024

PROBLEM STATEMENT
  • Urban planners and insurance companies need accurate roof material classification from aerial imagery
  • Manual inspection is expensive and unscalable across large urban areas
  • Existing object detection models lacked material-level classification precision for rooftops
SOLUTION
  • Fine-tuned YOLOv8 on custom-annotated aerial imagery dataset for roof material classification
  • Optimized for real-world deployment in insurance underwriting and disaster preparedness
  • Achieved significant accuracy improvement over baseline models
OUR POSITIONING
  • First application of YOLOv8 to roof material classification from aerial imagery
  • Practical utility for insurance risk assessment and municipal infrastructure planning
  • Demonstrated that careful problem selection yields high-impact results
ARCHITECTURE
  • YOLOv8 backbone with transfer learning from COCO pre-trained weights
  • Custom annotated dataset of aerial roof imagery with material labels
  • Data augmentation pipeline (rotation, scaling, color jitter)
  • Hyperparameter optimization for small object detection in aerial scenes
DATASET USED
  • Custom annotated aerial/satellite imagery dataset
  • Multiple roof material classes
  • Train/validation/test splits for rigorous evaluation
RESULTS
  • Improved mAP by 32.5% over YOLOv5 baseline
  • Best Paper Award at IEEE ICCDS 2024
  • Demonstrated practical applicability for insurance and urban planning
AWARDS
  • Best Paper Award, IEEE ICCDS 2024
