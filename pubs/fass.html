FASS: FAITHFULNESS & ATTRIBUTION STABILITY SCORE FOR POST-HOC XAI METHODS

CVPR · 2026

PROBLEM STATEMENT
  • Post-hoc XAI methods (SHAP, LIME, Grad-CAM, Integrated Gradients) lack standardized stability evaluation
  • Explanations can change dramatically under minor input perturbations, undermining trust
  • No existing benchmark measured faithfulness and attribution stability together across datasets
SOLUTION
  • Designed FASS (Faithfulness & Attribution Stability Score), a unified metric combining multiple stability dimensions
  • Evaluated 6+ XAI methods across systematic perturbation families (noise, rotation)
  • Built a reproducible benchmark pipeline with open-sourced code and results
OUR POSITIONING
  • First benchmark to jointly enforce prediction invariance as a precondition for attribution stability measurement
  • Reports retention rates as a first-class diagnostic quantity alongside stability scores
  • Three-component stability decomposition (SSIM, Spearman rank correlation, top-k Jaccard overlap) enabling targeted diagnosis of attribution failure modes
  • Cross-perturbation, cross-architecture, and cross-dataset baseline measurements establishing reference stability scores
ARCHITECTURE
  • PyTorch-based pipeline with Captum integration for gradient methods
  • SHAP and LIME wrappers for perturbation-based methods
  • Automated perturbation generation (Gaussian noise, rotations) across CIFAR-10, COCO, ImageNet
  • Component-level and aggregate FASS score computation with ablation support
DATASET USED
  • CIFAR-10: 10,000 images (32×32), bilinearly upsampled to 224×224 across 10 classes
  • ImageNet-1K: Subset of 40,000 high-resolution natural images across 1,000 categories
  • MS-COCO: Up to 30,000 images from the train2017 split spanning 80 object categories, classification head replaced with linear projection to 80 outputs
  • All images resized to 224×224 and normalized with ImageNet channel statistics
  • Total: ~70,000 images evaluated across 240 conditions (~6.4 million explanation computations)
RESULTS
  • Grad-CAM is the most stable method across all 12 dataset-architecture combinations
  • FASS composite scores: Grad-CAM 0.722 (ImageNet), 0.717 (CIFAR-10), 0.671 (COCO)
  • Method ranking Grad-CAM > IG > GradientSHAP > LIME preserved without rank crossings across all datasets
  • Method choice dominates architecture choice: Grad-CAM vs. IG gap (~0.21) is roughly twice the largest within-method architecture spread (~0.09)
  • Geometric perturbations consistently more destabilizing than photometric or compression perturbations
