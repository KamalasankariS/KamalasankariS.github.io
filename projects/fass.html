FASS: XAI STABILITY BENCHMARK

Faithfulness meets attribution. Every pixel accounted for.

SITUATION
  • Post-hoc XAI methods (SHAP, LIME, Grad-CAM, Integrated Gradients) are widely used to explain deep learning predictions
  • No standardized benchmark existed to evaluate whether these explanations remain stable under real-world perturbations
  • Models deployed in high-stakes domains (medical imaging, autonomous driving) need reliable, consistent explanations
TASK
  • Design and build a comprehensive stability benchmark across multiple datasets (CIFAR-10, COCO, ImageNet) and perturbation types (noise, rotation)
  • Evaluate 6+ XAI methods using multiple stability metrics (Average Sensitivity, Max Sensitivity, Rank Correlation, Attribution Overlap)
  • Produce actionable findings for practitioners choosing XAI methods for production systems
ACTION
  • Built full experimental pipeline in PyTorch with Captum, SHAP, and LIME integrations
  • Evaluated across 80K+ images spanning three datasets and two perturbation families
  • Computed component-level and aggregate FASS scores for each method-model-dataset combination
  • Ran ablation studies to isolate metric contributions
RESULT
  • Published at CVPR 2026
  • Revealed that gradient-based methods (Integrated Gradients) are significantly more stable than perturbation-based methods (LIME) under noise
  • Open-sourced the full benchmark pipeline and results
  • Adopted by researchers as a reference stability evaluation framework
